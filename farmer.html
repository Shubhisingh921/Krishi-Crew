कृषि-CREW

iPlow. iPlant. iHarvest.

"KRISHI CREW" is like a helpful friend to farmers in Jammu and Kashmir, offering a range of advantages. This online portal provides three main services to make farming easier and more successful. First, it helps farmers identify and deal with pests and diseases that can harm their crops, ensuring healthier harvests. Second, it predicts how well crops will do in the upcoming season, so farmers can plan better and know what to expect, which can save them time and resources. It even helps farmers find out what crops are in demand in the market, ensuring they grow the right things. Third, it connects farmers with buyers and helps them sell their crops at good prices, increasing their income. It also shares the latest weather forecasts and useful information about farming. KRISHI CREW is like a farmer's best buddy, offering support, guidance, and many advantages for a successful farming journey.

NPK DETECTION

NPK (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium) detection, when combined with environmental factors like rainfall, temperature, pH, and humidity, plays a pivotal role in revolutionizing agriculture in Jammu and Kashmir. By assessing soil nutrient levels, farmers can fine-tune fertilizer use, promoting healthier crop growth and maximizing yield. This approach aids in crop selection, rotation, and the efficient utilization of resources, as NPK data is intrinsically linked with environmental conditions. Additionally, data-driven decision-making, predictive analytics, and sustainable practices are made possible, leading to precise yield predictions and contributing to food security, while safeguarding the region's agricultural sustainability and prosperity.

INSURANCE POLICIES

Extending insurance policies to farmers in Jammu and Kashmir is a pivotal measure to safeguard their agricultural endeavors from the inherent risks of the region. Tailored insurance schemes can address the unique challenges faced by farmers, including unpredictable weather patterns, natural calamities, and crop-specific vulnerabilities. These policies encompass weather-based, yield-based, and livestock insurance, as well as coverage for agricultural equipment and infrastructure. Furthermore, they can include health insurance for farmers and income protection to shield against income fluctuations. Collaborative efforts between the government, insurance providers, and agricultural experts are essential in designing customized insurance initiatives that are accessible and financially viable for farmers, especially those in remote areas. By securing their livelihoods, these policies not only protect farmers but also incentivize agricultural modernization and contribute to the sustainable development of Jammu and Kashmir's agriculture sector.

NEWS

Dr Arun Kumar Mehta

Chief Secretary

J&K has favourable climatic conditions and immense untapped potential in the agriculture sector and with the right approach and endeavours, it can easily evolve as the Agri-capital of India.Dr. Mehta was speaking at 24th Foundation Day of Sher-e-Kashmir University of Agricultural Sciences and Technology of Jammu (SKUAST-J) held at its at Baba Jitto Auditorium, Main campus, Chatha The Chief Secretary underlined that around seventy percent of the population of J&K depends directly or indirectly on agriculture for their livelihoods. Development and promotion of the sector is vital for the prosperity of its people, he said. Dr Mehta highlighted that J&K stands top among other states in farmer-household income and called for further exploring ways to increase it. Due to the fast paced development done in the UT since 2019, we already stand among the top five states in various sectors.
